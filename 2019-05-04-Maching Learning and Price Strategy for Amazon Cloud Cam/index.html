Maching Learning and Price Strategy for Amazon Cloud Cam

2019-05-04   DATA


Customer reviews contain a large amount of information. One of the recurring subjects of NLP is to understand customer opinion through statement analysis of customer reviews. However, Basic Sentimental Classification can only tell customers’ overall impression about the product, and it can’t tell customers’ opinion of specific features of the product. But, if we dive deeper into the customer reviews, we can get more information.

My goal is to extract features from reviews, identify the opinion of each feature, quantify the sentiment using econometrics, and then generate a price strategy based on it.

1. Introduction

1.1 Sentiment Analysis

Before going into the methodology, let us refresh Sentiment Analysis. The Sentiment Analysis is a process of understanding the opinion of a piece of a subject (text or speech) is positive, neutral or negative. It is in the field of Nature Language Processing (NLP). Several machine learning methods can solve this problem, such as Logistic Regression, Support Vector Machines (SVM), Naive Bayes, and more. There are also many Python packages and APIs of Several Cloud providers (Google Cloud, AWS, …) can help us on Sentiment Analysis.

1.2 Basic Sentiment Classification

Basic Sentiment Classification classifies full documents, such as reviews, articles, based on the overall sentiment. The sentiment is majorly based on sentiment words (good, bad, great) or specific expressions.
However, a product review classified as negative sentiment does not mean that the customer doesn’t like any features and vice versa.

1.3 Feature Based Sentiment Analysis

To have a clear idea of what a customer like and dislike of a product,
We can extract feature and find the customer’s opinions related to these features.

Data Overview

The product category I was working on is the home security camera. I scraped reviews of targe product and its competitors from Amazon.com. The dataset contents 53,657 reviews of 15 different home security cameras from Amazon.com. with the following information for each one:

Name Description
Product Name of the Product
Username User’s ID
Stars 1-5 rating for the product
Title The subject of the review
Date The review date
Review Review text
Helpfulcount How many people click “Helpful” button
ReviewTotal How many comments under this review

The average length of reviews of the product is around 200.

Most of reviews is in the range of 4 years.

Most of the product has 2000 reviews.
